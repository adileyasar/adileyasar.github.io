Fata Morgana

I saw you shining in the heat, A figure drifting, soft, complete. You looked like peace, or something near — So I ran fast, without my fear.

Each step was fire beneath my feet, But I believed the lie looked sweet. I gave the chase my every part, A thirst for hope, a burning heart.

And when I reached where you should be, There was no touch, no remedy. Just heat and dust and silence wide — No hands, no voice, no place to hide.

I stood alone, the silence wide, No dream, no echo left to guide. The truth was not in what I’d chased — I ran so I wouldn’t face my face.
